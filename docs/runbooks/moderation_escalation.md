# Moderation Escalation

## Immediate safety

For credible threats, child-safety concerns, non-consensual intimate imagery, doxxing, or imminent
physical harm, preserve the report/case identifiers and escalate immediately to the designated
trust-and-safety/legal channel. Do not download, repost, or copy sensitive screenshots into chat,
tickets, or personal devices. Follow jurisdiction-specific preservation/reporting duties approved
by counsel; this runbook is operational guidance, not legal advice.

## Workflow

1. Open the moderation case and safe content preview. Confirm report reason, visibility, prior cases,
   account state, and whether sensitive-information detection warned on the screenshot.
2. Assign the case, set priority, and record concise internal notes. Use IDs and factual observations;
   avoid unnecessary reproduction of personal or illegal content.
3. Apply the least action that contains harm: remove recommendation eligibility, remove content,
   restrict posting/interactions/messaging, suspend, or ban. State a clear reason; every action is
   audited. Blocking or private visibility is not a moderation substitute.
4. Escalate uncertain high-severity cases to a second reviewer. Never restore high-risk content based
   only on an uploader assertion.
5. If compromise is suspected, follow the account-compromise runbook as well. If deletion is
   requested or legally required, coordinate with the deletion-incident runbook and preservation
   obligations before purging.

Record decision, policy basis, reviewer, timestamps, user notification decision, appeal route, and
external escalation reference. Restrict access to staff with a genuine need to know.
